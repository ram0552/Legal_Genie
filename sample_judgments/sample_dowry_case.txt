SUPREME COURT OF INDIA
CRIMINAL APPEAL NO. 5678 OF 2023

Ramesh Kumar ... Appellant
vs.
State of Gujarat ... Respondent

JUDGMENT

1. This criminal appeal arises from the judgment and order dated 10th May, 2023 passed by the High Court of Gujarat at Ahmedabad in Criminal Appeal No. 234 of 2022, whereby the High Court upheld the conviction of the appellant under Section 304B of the Indian Penal Code (Dowry Death).

2. The brief facts of the case are as follows:

3. The deceased, Priya Devi, was married to the appellant on 15th March, 2019. Within two years of marriage, on 20th July, 2021, she was found dead at her matrimonial home. The post-mortem report revealed death by hanging.

4. The prosecution alleged that the deceased was subjected to continuous harassment and cruelty by the appellant and his family members for additional dowry. The parents of the deceased testified that their daughter had complained about dowry demands during visits to her parental home.

5. Under Section 304B of the IPC, the essential ingredients to be established are:
   (a) Death of a woman within seven years of marriage
   (b) Death was not under normal circumstances
   (c) Soon before death, she was subjected to cruelty or harassment
   (d) Such cruelty or harassment was in connection with demand for dowry

6. The presumption under Section 113B of the Indian Evidence Act, 1872 comes into play when the conditions of Section 304B are fulfilled. The burden then shifts to the accused to rebut the presumption.

7. In the present case, we have examined the evidence on record. The dying declaration made by the deceased to her mother about dowry harassment has been given due weight. Multiple witnesses from the neighborhood corroborated the ill-treatment.

8. The defense contention that the death was a simple suicide cannot be accepted in light of the evidence establishing dowry harassment. As held in Kamesh Panjiyar v. State of Bihar (2005) 2 SCC 388, once harassment for dowry is established and unnatural death occurs within seven years, the presumption operates against the accused.

9. We have also considered Article 21 of the Constitution which guarantees right to life with dignity. Women's rights to live free from harassment is a fundamental aspect of this constitutional guarantee.

10. For the foregoing reasons, we find no merit in this appeal. The conviction and sentence imposed by the courts below is hereby affirmed.

11. The appeal is accordingly dismissed.
